2013-2014 NBA season

The 2013-2014 season was a season of change. On one side, you have rising teams like the Golden State Warriors and the Houston Rockets. On the other hand, there was a clear decline of the Miami Heat and the San Antonio Spurs. As their stars aged, they were no longer the dominant forces they once were.

Despite their age, the Miami Heat and the San Antonio Spurs made their way back to the finals to face off against one another for the second year in a row. But for the Heat, Dwayne Wade and Chris Bosh's age has started showing and, despite the heroic efforts of LeBron James, the Spurs beat the Heat in 5

The Finals MVP wasn't Tim Duncan, or Tony Parker, it was Kawhi Leonard, a young 2-way beast who dominated all series and has earned his right to call himself a superstar

2014 NBA OFFSEASON

The 2014 offseason was a season of change. The biggest move of the offseason was the signing of LeBron James to the Cleveland Cavaliers, reuniting him with his former teammate, Kyrie Irving. This move was seen as a way for the Cavaliers to become a title contender, and it would pay off in the future.

Other notable moves include Kevin Love being traded to the Cavaliers, and Carmelo Anthony being traded to the New York Knicks.
