2012-2013 NBA season

The 2012-2013 season was an exciting season, but for the Los Angeles lakers, it was nothing short of dissapointing. The team everyone expected to be a title contender were nothing more than a fringe afterthought. To make things worse, Kobe Bryant suffered an Achillies tear, making him unavailable for the playoffs. Without him, Lakers stood no chance.

The primary focus was on 3 teams, Miami Heat, San Antonio Spurs, and Olahoma City Thunder.

The NBA finals were the Miami Heat vs San Antonio Spurs. A matchup everyone anticipated saw the Heat go down 3-2, going to game 6 back in South Beach. With seconds left, the Heat were down 3. Then....


The shot that sent shockwaves through the NBA world. Ray Allen hitting that 3 forced overtime, where the Heat won to force a game 7 back on their home floor. The Miami Heat won the Game 7 with a nail-bitter, with LeBron James winning his second Finals MVP, finishing a historic season.

This season also saw the emergence of Stephen Curry as a superstar, but injuries have prevented him from making a real impact.. for now

2013 NBA OFFSEASON

The 2013 offseason saw Andre Iguondala join the Golden State Warriors, a move that would pay huge dividends in the future. he Miami Heat also re-signed Ray Allen, a move that would prove to be crucial in their quest for back-to-back championships. But the biggest shock of the Offseason saw Kevin Garnett and Paul Pierce being traded to the Brooklyn Nets, which saw the Boston Celtics getting back a haul of draft picks that would be turned into their next championship [foreshadowing]

But the biggest move of the offseason was the trade that sent James Harden to the Houston Rockets. This move was seen as a way for the Rockets to become a title contender, and it would pay off in the future.
