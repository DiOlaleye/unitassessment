2015-2016 NBA season

The 2015-2016 season was a historic season for the Golden State Warriors, at they broke the single season win record, finishing with a 73-9 record. Steph Curry won his second MVP award, becoming the first unanimous MVP in NBA history. Other notable players this season were Kawhi Leonard, who won his first Defensive Player of the Year award, and Russell Westbrook, who averaged a triple-double for the entire season.

The playoffs saw the Warriors make their way to the finals to face off against the Cleveland Cavaliers, a team that was looking for redemption after being swept in the previous year's finals. The Cavaliers were led by LeBron James and Kyrie Irving, who were looking to bring a championship to Cleveland for the first time in franchise history.

The Warriors took a 3-1 lead in the series, but the Cavaliers made a historic comeback, with Kyrie and LeBron both scoring 41 each winning the next three games to win the series in 7 games. LeBron James was named Finals MVP for his outstanding performance throughout the series, leading both teams in points, rebounds and assists.
