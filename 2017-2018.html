2017-2018 NBA season

The 2017-2018 season saw the Golden State Warriors continue their reign of terror, but they finished second in the West Behind the Houston Rockets, who finished with a 65-17 record. The Rockets were led by the MVP James Harden, and his sidekick Chris Paul, who formed a deadly duo that was unstoppable on offense.

The playoffs saw the Rockets and Warriors meet in the Western Conference Finals, and it was one of the most competitive series in recent memory. The Rockets took a 3-2 lead in the series, but the Warriors came back to win the next two games to win the series in 7 games.

Over in the East, without Kyrie Irving, Lebron James did all the heavy lifting for his team. He battled the Indiana Pacers in 7 games, Swept the Toronto Raptors in 4, (earning them the nickname "LeBronto") and beat the Boston Celtics in 7 games to reach the finals.

The finals was a rematch of the previous year, but this one was even less competitive, as the Warriors swept the Cavaliers in 4 games. Kevin Durant was named Finals MVP for the second year in a row, and the Warriors cemented their legacy as one of the greatest teams in NBA history.

2018 NBA OFFSEASON

The biggest move of the 2018 offseason was Kawhi Leonard being traded to the Toronto Raptors, a move that would pay huge dividends in the future. Other notable moves include DeAndre Jordan being traded to the Dallas Mavericks, and Jimmy Butler being traded to the Philadelphia 76ers.
